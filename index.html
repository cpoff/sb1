Where the mountains meet the fairways

Twenty-five miles north of downtown Tucson, Arizona, SaddleBrooke One beckons you with a tranquil desert setting and resort amenities: a 27-hole golf course, tennis center, pickleball, bocce, state-of-the-art gyms and much more.

Your days will be as busy as you want them to be, or you can relax with a book at our pool with spa or our patio with stunning mountain views.

Late afternoon finds our residents and guests socializing in the Agave Lounge for happy hour as the sun casts a soft pink glow on the Catalina Mountains. Dining options are available from breakfast to dinner at our restaurant and grill, each with panoramic mountain and fairway vistas.

Located at 3,500 feet above sea level, SaddleBrooke enjoys mountain breezes that mix with warm desert days and crisp evenings under sparkling starry skies. Deer, quail, roadrunners and coyotes add to the natural beauty of this oasis. SaddleBrooke residents take great pride in our community with its well maintained common areas and amenities. Come and see for yourself.

SaddleBrooke One is a Robson master-planned adult community founded in 1987 on 1,200 acres with approximately 3,650 residents, 2,000 homes, and 26 miles of private roads. SaddleBrooke One transitioned from developer control in 1999 and is governed by a seven-member Board of Directors in accordance with the CC&Rs and bylaws. The day-to-day operations are overseen by professional managers who are employees of the association.

Golf

Nestled against the foothills of the Santa Catalina Mountains, SaddleBrooke One provides residents and guests with 27 holes of park-land style golf featuring undulating fairways and tall native trees. There are three courses: SaddleBrooke, Tucson and Catalina, which may be played in any combination to create a unique golf experience.

Tucson Nine
Renovated in 2011, the Tucson Nine is regarded as the most challenging of the three courses in SaddleBrooke. The course offers stunning views of the Catalina Mountain range from every hole. This nine is highlighted by hole #4, a strong par 4 requiring a tee shot over a pond to a sloping fairway with gorgeous mountain views in the background. The course challenge lies in the deep bunkers which await an errant shot and the length of the course.

SaddleBrooke Nine
Renovated in 2010, the SaddleBrooke Nine offers westward views of spectacular sunsets. This course is characterized not by length but rather by placing a premium on accuracy as is evidence by the signature hole #2 – only 125 yards in length requiring all carry over a pond. The SaddleBrooke Nine is the most requested nine for its fair yet challenging layout.

Catalina Nine
The newest of all three nines, the Catalina course features three par 3 holes and plays to a par of 35. The course wanders through natural arroyos and brings the golfer close up views of the Catalina Mountain range. The signature hole #5 is a risk-reward par 5 featuring a second shot which must carry 250 yards to clear the ditch in the fairway. The Catalina Nine offers a wide variety of golf shots and is regarded among residents as the best layout of the three courses.

Tennis

Centrally located at the southeast corner of SaddleBrooke Blvd and Ridgeview, the Tennis Center is the hub of tennis activities. With its large patio, gas grill, ice machine, bulletin boards and restrooms, the Tennis Center is the place to gather for social events and watch tennis in the shade. You can reserve the patio for other activities such as parties. The office has TV, coffee and comfy places to sit.

The Tennis Club actively manages the tennis court usage within SaddleBrooke (Eight courts at the SaddleBrooke Tennis Center and 11 other courts located within SaddleBrooke). Membership in the SaddleBrooke Tennis Club is open to all residents of SaddleBrooke but membership is not a requirement to use the courts.

Dining

Subhed

Within the SaddleBrooke community, you will find seven restaurants for dining choices from morning to evening. At SaddleBrooke One, the casual RoadRunner Grill and patio offers breakfast and lunch. There are themed evenings and special events scheduled on the patio which overlooks the golf course. Gourmet pizza is found at Brooke One Pizza Kitchen.

For your evening dining pleasure, the place to relax and order from a wide-selection of menu items is upstairs in The Vistas Dining and The Agave Lounge with its twilight views of the Catalina Mountains. You can even choose to order meals to go, seven days a week. Menus are updated frequently and posted to the Residents Website.

Frequently offered, special culinary events feature wine tasting, creative menus and themed dinners. Meet your friends for dinner, plan a special event dinner or just stop by for a spur-of-the-moment snack. All of that is within easy reach at our SaddleBrooke restaurants.

A reciprocal use agreement with SaddleBrooke Two rounds out the restaurant options with a the MountainView Bistro, Mesquite Grill and Preserve Restaurant.

Patrol One

SaddleBrooke patrols keep watch. They are not law enforcement officers, but they regulate traffic and help keep the community safer. The enforcement of the SaddleBrooke One rules is aided by the patrols. They respond to incidents, accidents, and injury reports. As an additional courtesy, they maintain a confidential list of emergency contacts supplied by the home owners, perform home checks at the request of residents, and notify residents when they have inadvertently left their garage doors open late at night.

Fire and Police

SaddleBrooke is served by Golder Ranch Fire District. There are two fire stations within SaddleBrooke that are staffed 24/7. In addition to responding to fire and emergency medical calls, the firemen graciously give their time to educate the residents on safety issues, remove unwanted desert creatures from yards, change light bulbs in hard to reach vaulted ceilings, and replace batteries in smoke detectors.

Deputies of the Pinal County Sheriff are frequently seen patrolling SaddleBrooke in addition to responding to calls for assistance. They maintain a substation in the SaddleBrooke business center.

Services in Tucson

Downtown Tucson is just 25 miles away. Here you will find the University of Arizona, Tucson Symphony, live theatres, Tucson Music Hall, sporting events, museums, botanical gardens and a lot more.

The Tucson International Airport is approximately an hour away. Six airlines service the airport with 60 departures a day to 18 non-stop destinations. The parking rates are some of the lowest you will find.
